TurkeyHong Kong

Abstract

This website showcases a conceptual design of a cyberpunk game universe, inspired by Hong Kong and Turkey’s current political and urban issues.


The cyberpunk theme is typically associated with dystopian societies, and both Hong Kong and Turkey show a trend of descending towards authoritarian rulership. Hong Kong citizens are losing their autonomy to the extremely centralized government of China, whereas the Turkish government had become an authoritarian regime. Both regions now suffer from the suppression of press freedoms and freedom of speech. Abductions, arrests, and in certain cases even murders of journalists and human rights activists had become common occurrences, and censorship of media happens everyday, suppressing conflicting points of view.
The future scenario depicted here is a study on the culture, urbanism and history of the two regions, combined to form a dystopian fiction that drives the theme of the game universe. The four different aspects showcased here tackle four points of design: illustrations of the abstract symbolism and concepts that are present in a dystopian society; constructions of the architecture and urbanism behind the fictional city; narratives within society from the urban poor and the disadvantaged; and the analytical point of view of a journalist within this society.
The illustrations are concept art and abstract interpretations of the current societal and urban issues within the two regions: these concept arts overlap the two regions as one to create a new imagery that merge the two regions to form the dystopian theme that drives this whole website.
The concepts of the architecture within this fictional universe refer back to the historical and contemporary styles of both Hong Kong and Turkey. These styles refer to events that are dystopian in nature but not in appearance, showing the utopian facade that is portrayed to the citizens of the regions.
The stories of the urban poor and disadvantaged show what citizens seek in their actions against the government and portray their daily struggles against the oppression. This part takes inspiration from the informal settlements such as Kowloon Walled City and historical Cappadocia.
The final point of view reflects the events that has lead up to the dystopian society in a recount, showcasing the histories that lead up to the point the fictional universe is situated in, taking inspiration from the events like the 1980 Turkey coup d’etat, the 2013 Gezi Park protests and the 2014 Hong Kong Umbrella Movement.
